❓ How to play

🚶 Walk inside (first person — like Minecraft)

  • 🕹️ WASD or arrow keys to walk, mouse to look
  • 🖱️ Left-click place a block • Right-click break a block • Middle-click copy a block
  • ⬆️ Space jump • tap Space twice to fly (Space up, Shift down)
  • 🎨 Scroll wheel or number keys 1–9, 0 pick a color • F paints the block you're looking at
  • 🔺 Q cycles Cube → Wedge → Round → Curve • R turns the block • G cycles Full → Half → Quarter size
  • 🔓 Esc frees the mouse to use the buttons; click the world to dive back in

🎥 Camera view (spin around from outside)

  • 🧱 Pick Build / Erase / Paint, then click the ground or a block
  • 🔄 Drag to spin, scroll to zoom, right-drag to slide • quick right-click erases

🔺 Shapes & sizes (both views)

  • 🧱 Cube, 🔺 Wedge (45° sloped edge), Round (rounds a corner seen from above — stack them for round towers!), or Curve (a wedge with a rounded slope) — pick from the shape bar or press Q to cycle
  • 🔄 Turn (or press R) spins a shape to face any of four directions — the icons spin too, so you always know which way they point
  • 🔍 The Size button (or G) cycles Full → Half → Quarter for the next block — mix sizes to add fine details to big builds. Half blocks snap to a half grid, quarter blocks to a quarter grid. The button lights up when you're building small.

✨ Everywhere

  • 🦋 Mirror (M): build both sides at once — wedges flip to mirror too, great for roofs and ships!
  • ↩️ Ctrl+Z undo, Ctrl+Shift+Z redo • 📦 My Stuff saves your builds

👨‍🔧 For grown-ups: printing tips

  • “Print It!” downloads a watertight binary STL, already rotated Z-up and sitting on the bed — drop it straight into your slicer.
  • ✨ Soft edges adds a tiny (0.3 mm) bevel along every exposed block edge — corners print less sharp, and block seams show as fine grooves so the model keeps its built-from-bricks look. Turn it off for perfectly flat, seamless surfaces.
  • The size chips set the edge length of one block (3 / 5 / 8 mm). The dialog shows the final printed size in cm.
  • Floating blocks (not connected to the ground) print as separate loose pieces — the export dialog warns about them and can highlight them.
  • Big overhangs may need supports in your slicer, just like any print.
  • Colors are for fun on screen — the STL is a single solid. Pause-at-height still works great for layered color!
  • Everything is saved in this browser automatically (plus 📦 My Stuff for keepers). No internet needed.
